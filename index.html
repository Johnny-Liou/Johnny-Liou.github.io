Ze-Wei (Johnny) Liou 劉則暐

Research Assistant, Academia Sinica | B.S. Electrical Engineering, National Taiwan University

zwljohnny@gmail.com

I received my Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering from National Taiwan University in June 2024. Currently, I am working as a research assistant at Academia Sinica, Taiwan’s national academic institution, under the guidance of Dr. Ding-Yong Hong and Dr. Jan-Jan Wu. I have had the privilege of interning at IBM Research Almaden, where I was mentored by Dr. Geoffrey Burr and Dr. Hsin-Yu (Sidney) Tsai, and working as an undergraduate researcher at NTU Access Lab, advised by Prof. An-Yeu (Andy) Wu.

My research focuses on hardware/software co-design for AI accelerators, particularly in Computing-In-Memory (CIM) circuits and multicore inference accelerators. My work includes CIM architecture design, CIM-aware quantization, algorithms for dynamic resource allocation on multicore accelerators, and full-stack accelerator design for N:M structured pruning. I am also experienced in and interested in digital VLSI system design. I like to design new stuff and test unconventional ideas. In my free time, I like to play Go, basketball, and badminton.
